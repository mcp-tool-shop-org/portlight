# Portlight — World Handbook

> Canonical lore reference. Every name, grudge, and trade relationship in this
> document is **canon**. New content must be consistent with what's written here.
> When in doubt, the handbook wins.

---

## The Known World

Five regions connected by sea lanes of increasing danger and reward. A 360-day
year cycles through four seasons. The calendar shapes trade as surely as the wind.

### Seasons

| Season | Days | Character |
|--------|------|-----------|
| **Spring** | 1–90 | Calm seas, trade reopens. The Mediterranean wakes. Ice breaks in the North. |
| **Summer** | 91–180 | Peak activity. Monsoon devastates East Indies. Typhoons rake the South Seas. North Atlantic golden window. |
| **Autumn** | 181–270 | Harvest season. Grain floods the Mediterranean. Storms build. Pre-winter stockpiling in the North. |
| **Winter** | 271–360 | Harsh. North Atlantic deadly (1.8x danger). Medicine demand doubles. The South Seas are gentle. |

## Regions

### Mediterranean — "The Middle Sea"

**Ethos:** Civilization is commerce. The law of contract binds harder than iron.

**Proverb:** *"A contract honored is worth more than gold hoarded."*

The cradle of trade. Established routes, moderate prices, and the safest waters
for new captains. The Exchange Alliance (Porto Novo, Al-Manar, Silva Bay) sets
the rules. Corsair's Rest exists in the cracks between those rules.

**Sacred good:** Grain — never let a city starve.

**Seasonal rhythm:** Autumn grain harvest floods markets. Winter drives medicine
and tea demand. Spring is the gentlest sailing.

**Pirate faction:** The Crimson Tide (organized, militaristic, protection racket).
Based at Corsair's Rest.

---

### North Atlantic — "The Iron Coast"

**Ethos:** Strength is built, not born. Everything here is made to endure.

**Proverb:** *"The storm tests the hull, not the cargo."*

Industrial north — iron, weapons, and storm-battered coastlines. The Iron Pact
(Ironhaven, Stormwall) controls the weapons trade. Thornport stays neutral,
selling tea and tobacco to anyone with silver.

**Sacred good:** Medicines — the north remembers its plagues.

**Seasonal rhythm:** Summer is the golden window (0.7x danger, 1.15x speed).
Winter is lethal (1.8x danger, 0.7x speed). Medicine demand peaks in winter.

**Pirate faction:** The Iron Wolves (ex-garrison soldiers, professional and ruthless).
Nomadic — no port loyalty.

---

### West Africa — "The Gold Coast"

**Ethos:** The land gives freely to those who give back. Trade is relationship.

**Proverb:** *"The market remembers who gave, not who took."*

Rich oral tradition, communal commerce. The Gold Coast Compact (Sun Harbor,
Palm Cove, Iron Point, Pearl Shallows) operates on generosity and long-term
reciprocity. They distrust industrial powers but welcome honest traders.

**Sacred good:** Pearls — diving is a sacred craft.

**Seasonal rhythm:** Dry season (winter) is best for mining and calm seas.
Rainy season (summer) favors pearl diving. Dyer's Moon festival peaks in
the dry months.

**Pirate faction:** The Deep Reef Brotherhood (honor-bound anarchists).
Nomadic — territory spans South Seas and West Africa.

---

### East Indies — "The Silk Waters"

**Ethos:** Patience is mastery. Everything here was old when your civilization was young.

**Proverb:** *"A merchant who rushes drinks salt water."*

Ancient, sophisticated, hierarchical. The Silk Circle (Jade Port, Silk Haven,
Dragon's Gate) controls porcelain, silk, and the finest tea. Weapons are
forbidden. The Monsoon Syndicate operates from Spice Narrows in the shadows.

**Sacred goods:** Porcelain, silk — master craftsmen are revered.
**Forbidden good:** Weapons — banned by the Gate Wardens at Dragon's Gate.

**Seasonal rhythm:** Monsoon (summer) is devastating (1.7x danger). Post-monsoon
autumn is the spice harvest — best buying window. Winter dry season is peak
silk/porcelain production.

**Pirate faction:** The Monsoon Syndicate (political, hierarchical, information-driven).
Based at Spice Narrows.

---

### South Seas — "The Reef Kingdoms"

**Ethos:** The sea gives life and takes it back. Trade here is tribute.

**Proverb:** *"The reef welcomes the patient; it drowns the greedy."*

Island kingdoms, volcanic, fiercely independent. The Coral Crown (Ember Isle,
Typhoon Anchorage, Coral Throne) answers to the Coral King. Trade is tribute —
offered willingly or not at all. Weapons and silk are the currencies of respect.

**Sacred good:** Pearls — gift from the sea.

**Seasonal rhythm:** Typhoon season (summer) is dangerous (1.5x). Winter is
the gentlest season. Coral Coronation festival drives weapons/silk demand.

**Pirate faction:** The Deep Reef Brotherhood also operates here.

## The 20 Ports

### 1. Porto Novo (Mediterranean)

**Trade bloc:** Exchange Alliance (heart)
**Cultural group:** The Exchange Guild
**Features:** Shipyard

**Identity:** Gateway to inland trade. Grain ships fill the docks. The Exchange
Guild sets dawn rates and arbitrates disputes. Porto Novo IS the Alliance —
the headquarters, the policy, the standard weights and measures.

**Landmark:** The Grain Exchange — a columned hall where prices are read aloud at dawn.

**Local custom:** Captains ring the harbor bell on first arrival. It's bad luck not to.

**Dock scene:** Grain barges jostle for position along the stone quays. Clerks
with wax tablets tally every bushel while porters sweat under heavy sacks.

**Tavern rumor:** They say the Grain Exchange is built on the ruins of an older
temple. Some nights the clerks hear chanting from beneath the floor.

**Political stance:** Porto Novo is the Alliance's heart. The Exchange Guild
sets policy here.

**Local rivalry:** Al-Manar. Both claim to be the Mediterranean's premier trading
port — the grain exchange vs. the spice bazaar. Centuries of competition.

**Port grudge:** Al-Manar once tried to impose a spice tariff on Porto Novo
grain ships. Porto Novo responded by raising port fees for Al-Manar flagged
vessels. The tariff lasted three days.

**Exports:** Grain (primary), rum
**Wants:** Cotton, dyes

#### Institutions

**The Harbor Master's Tower** — Stone tower at the harbor mouth. Brass telescope
on the roof, ledger room that smells of ink and sealing wax.
- **Harbor Master Vasco da Reira** (meticulous) — Has logged every ship for 31 years.
  Knows your tonnage before you drop anchor. Despises smugglers, respects clean paperwork.
  *"Manifest and tonnage, Captain. I'll assign your berth once I've reviewed them."*

**The Grain Exchange** — Columned hall on the waterfront, largest building in port.
Dawn rates read from the steps every morning.
- **Guild Factor Marta Soares** (shrewd) — The woman who reads the dawn rates.
  Never wrong about grain prices. Once cornered the entire Mediterranean cotton
  market for three weeks. Wants Porto Novo to remain the price-setting authority.
  *"You're here for the rates? They were posted at dawn."*

**Andros & Sons Shipyard** — Sprawling yard of slipways and dry docks. Three hulls
always under construction. The sons are long grown — just Dimitri and his apprentices.
- **Master Shipwright Dimitri Andros** (gruff) — Judges captains by their ship's
  condition, not their silver. Refused to build warships for the Iron Pact — earned
  the Brotherhood's loyalty. Told Scarlet Ana to build her flagship herself.
  *"Let me see your hull before we talk price. I don't repair what I wouldn't sail."*

**The Governor's Residence** — Modest stone house, distinguished only by the
Alliance banner. No throne — just a desk covered in charts and correspondence.
- **Port Governor Senhora Isabela Costa** (diplomatic) — Twelve years governing,
  longer than any predecessor. Former trader (ran the Al-Manar to Sun Harbor route
  for 15 years). Proposed the Alliance-wide weapons embargo against pirate-linked
  captains. Rumored to keep a ship hidden in Silva Bay, just in case.
  *"Welcome to Porto Novo, Captain. Our markets are fair and our laws clear."*

**The Harbor Bell** (tavern) — Built from ship timbers, salvaged bell over the door.
Too warm, too loud, too crowded. The food is terrible. The rum is excellent.
- **Old Enzo** (jovial) — Tavern keeper since before Vasco started keeping records.
  Knows everyone's secrets, tells none — unless the rum is good enough. Missing two
  fingers on his left hand. Was a pirate once, or so the story goes.
  *"Sit! Drink! First one's on the house — I'll put it on your next cargo."*

**The Customs House** — Clean whitewashed building, iron bars on every window,
locked evidence room in the basement.
- **Inspector Salva** (relentless) — Thin as a blade, memory for cargo that borders
  on supernatural. Never accepted a bribe. Brother was killed by smuggled black
  powder — every seizure is personal. Once inspected a ship for nine hours straight.
  *"Cargo manifest. All of it. I'll inspect the hold at my discretion."*

**The Broker's Desk** — Cramped office behind the Exchange. Walls covered in pinned
contracts and route maps.
- **Senior Broker Fernanda Reis** (calculating) — Tracks six contracts simultaneously
  without writing anything down. Takes a cut of everything she brokers. Steers good
  contracts toward reliable captains, leaves scraps for unknowns.
  *"New captain? I have contracts. Small ones, for now. Complete them on time and we'll talk."*

#### Power Dynamics

Power is triangulated: Costa sets policy, Marta controls prices, Salva enforces
the law. Dimitri's shipyard is independent — the Brotherhood answers to craft.
Enzo's tavern is the shadow channel where information flows that officials pretend
doesn't exist. Fernanda decides which contracts reach which captains.

**Core tension:** Marta vs. Costa. Marta wants market dominance; Costa wants
political stability. They need each other — Costa can't govern without Guild
revenue, Marta can't trade without Costa's protection. This tension keeps Porto
Novo honest.

### 2. Al-Manar (Mediterranean)

**Trade bloc:** Exchange Alliance (soul)
**Cultural group:** The Spice Merchants' Circle
**Features:** None (luxury hub)

**Identity:** Ancient port famed for its spice markets. The Spice Merchants'
Circle controls the auctions and guards trade secrets. Older than Porto Novo,
prouder, and convinced it should lead the Alliance.

**Landmark:** The Spice Bazaar — a covered market where a hundred aromas compete.

**Local custom:** Bargaining begins with tea. Refusing the first cup insults the seller.

**Dock scene:** Feluccas with triangular sails crowd the harbor. Porters carry
brass-bound chests of spice up marble steps worn smooth by centuries of trade.

**Tavern rumor:** A merchant from the east claims to know a spice island no
chart has ever shown. He's been buying a lot of rum and saying little else.

**Political stance:** Al-Manar is the Alliance's soul — older, prouder, and
convinced it should be in charge.

**Local rivalry:** Porto Novo. The Spice Merchants' Circle wants Al-Manar to
lead, not Porto Novo. The rivalry is genteel but real.

**Port grudge:** Porto Novo got the Alliance headquarters. Al-Manar got the
better harbor. Neither has forgiven the other.

**Exports:** Spice (primary), medicines
**Wants:** Grain (badly), porcelain, tea

#### Institutions

**The Harbor of Arrivals** — Curved stone quay lined with date palms, reception
pavilion where arriving captains receive dates and water before business.
- **Harbor Master Khalil al-Rashid** (ceremonial) — Treats every arrival as a
  diplomatic event. Once refused docking to a Porto Novo flagship for skipping
  the greeting ritual. The diplomatic incident lasted a month.
  *"Welcome to Al-Manar, Captain. Please — dates, water. We discuss your berth after you have rested."*

**The Spice Bazaar** — Three city blocks of covered market, brass lamps, dried
herb bundles. A hundred stalls. Auction floor at the center.
- **The Spice Mother, Yasmin al-Nadir** (imperious) — Fifty years judging spice.
  Can name an island, harvest year, and grower family from a single taste. She
  doesn't negotiate — she declares the price. Wants Al-Manar to lead the Alliance.
  *"Tell me what you carry, and I will tell you what it is worth. In Al-Manar, the price is the truth."*

**The Khoury Palace** — Tiled palace above the harbor. Mosaics of trade ships,
fountains, six generations of Khoury portraits. Business conducted from a divan.
- **Merchant Princess Nadia Khoury** (regal) — Hereditary ruler, six generations.
  Thinks in generations, not quarters. Secretly corresponding with the Silk Circle —
  could reshape the Alliance or shatter it.
  *"Al-Manar welcomes all who trade with honor. My house is open, Captain."*

**The Amber Glass** (tea house) — Low tables, silk cushions, amber glass windows
filtering harbor light into gold. Not a tavern — information flows over tea.
- **Old Farouk** (philosophical) — Ex-navigator, sailed the Monsoon Shortcut seven
  times. Retired saying he'd learned everything the sea could teach. Collects stories
  like Yasmin collects spices. He and Yasmin have argued about spice vs. tea for
  forty years.
  *"Sit. Tea first. Business is for those who have already tasted patience."*

**The Scales of Truth** (customs) — Marble building, inscription 'WEIGHT AND TRUTH.'
Brass scales of extraordinary precision. Confiscated counterfeits in glass cases.
- **Inspector Zara Osman** (precise) — Poached from Porto Novo by Nadia. Can identify
  fake saffron by smell. Cares more about fraud than smuggling — counterfeit spice
  is a worse crime than contraband here. Misses working with Inspector Salva.
  *"Cargo for inspection. I'm particularly interested in your spice lots."*

**The Apothecary House** — Whitewashed building, rooftop herb garden. Shelves of
labeled jars: roots, dried flowers, mineral powders. Unique to Al-Manar.
- **Apothecary Master Hakim al-Nadir** (gentle) — Yasmin's nephew. Inherited her
  nose for quality but not her ruthlessness. Turned down a fortune from the Iron
  Pact for exclusive medicine rights. Sells at fair prices, infuriating merchants.
  *"Do you carry medicines or ingredients? I'm always interested in what the sea brings."*

**The Broker's Alcove** — Arched alcove off the Bazaar, curtained with silk.
Rosewood desk. Contracts handwritten on parchment, not printed like Porto Novo's.
- **Senior Broker Tariq Sayed** (ambitious) — Youngest senior broker in Al-Manar's
  history. Appointed by Nadia, watched by everyone. Courting Silk Circle traders for
  exclusive luxury contracts that bypass Porto Novo entirely. If he succeeds, it
  shifts Alliance power. If he fails, Nadia will deny she supported him.
  *"I have contracts — good ones. Al-Manar doesn't waste time with small work."*

#### Power Dynamics

Hereditary governance — the Khoury family rules, not an elected governor. Real
power shared between Nadia (policy), Yasmin (market), and Hakim (medicines — the
quiet advantage). Old Farouk sees everything from his tea house. Zara protects
market integrity. Tariq pushes for expansion, supported by Nadia but watched by all.

**Core tension:** Tradition vs. ambition. Yasmin wants to preserve Al-Manar's
ancient way. Tariq wants to modernize with Silk Circle partnerships. Nadia plays
both sides — funding Tariq's ambitions while publicly deferring to Yasmin. Her
secret Silk Circle correspondence could reshape the Alliance or destroy it.

**Cross-port dynamics:** Inspector Zara was poached from Porto Novo (Salva's
former partner). Tariq is competing directly with Porto Novo's Fernanda Reis
for premium contracts. Nadia and Costa are political counterparts who respect
each other but represent incompatible visions of the Alliance's future.

### 3. Silva Bay (Mediterranean)

**Trade bloc:** Exchange Alliance (builder)
**Cultural group:** The Shipwrights' Brotherhood
**Features:** Shipyard (best in the region)

**Identity:** Timber-rich bay surrounded by dense forests. The shipwrights here
are the best in the Mediterranean — possibly the world. Silva Bay stays out of
politics and builds ships. The Alliance needs them too much to push.

**Landmark:** The Master Shipyard — where the finest hulls in the Mediterranean take shape.

**Local custom:** Touching another captain's ship without permission is grounds for a duel.

**Dock scene:** Sawdust drifts like snow across the waterfront. Half-finished
hulls rise on slipways, their ribs exposed like the skeletons of great fish.

**Tavern rumor:** The master shipwright refuses to build for anyone who has lost
a ship to storms. She says it's bad wood-luck.

**Political stance:** Silva Bay stays out of politics and builds ships. The
Alliance needs them too much to push.

**Port grudge:** The Shipwrights' Brotherhood refused to build warships for the
Iron Pact. Stormwall hasn't forgotten.

**Exports:** Timber (primary), iron
**Wants:** Cotton, weapons (shipyard needs)

---

### 4. Corsair's Rest (Mediterranean)

**Trade bloc:** Shadow Ports
**Cultural group:** The Brotherhood of the Cove
**Features:** Black Market
**Pirate faction base:** The Crimson Tide

**Identity:** A lawless harbor tucked between cliffs. Smugglers, pirates, and
those who trade with them. The Brotherhood of the Cove is not a guild — it's
a silence. They protect each other by knowing nothing.

**Landmark:** The Cliff Tavern — carved into the rock face, visible only from the sea.

**Local custom:** No names. Every captain here is "friend" until proven otherwise.

**Dock scene:** Ships anchor in the hidden cove with no flags flying. A boy in
a rowboat approaches to collect the docking fee — payable in silver, no questions.

**Tavern rumor:** There's a passage through the cliffs that leads to a second
harbor. Only the oldest pirates know the way, and they aren't talking.

**Political stance:** Corsair's Rest has no politics. It has silence, silver,
and the understanding that names are optional.

**Local rivalry:** Porto Novo. Corsair's Rest exists because Porto Novo's laws
are too strict. Every smuggler here is a trader Porto Novo drove away.

**Port grudge:** Porto Novo's harbor master publicly burned a cargo manifest
from Corsair's Rest. The Brotherhood took his favorite ship. He got it back —
without the cargo.

**Contraband available:** Black powder (primary), stolen cargo, opium (small)
**Exports:** Weapons, rum, tobacco
**Wants:** Silk, medicines

### 5. Ironhaven (North Atlantic)

**Trade bloc:** Iron Pact (core)
**Cultural group:** The Iron Guild
**Features:** Shipyard

**Identity:** Industrial port city wreathed in forge smoke. Weapons and iron
flow out, everything else flows in. The Iron Guild's word is law north of
the strait. The Great Foundry's chimney is visible twenty leagues at sea.

**Landmark:** The Great Foundry — its chimney visible twenty leagues at sea, glowing red at night.

**Local custom:** Greeting the harbor master with a nod, not words. The north wastes neither.

**Dock scene:** Iron-hulled barges sit low in the water. Cranes swing overhead,
loading ingots onto merchant vessels. Sparks drift like orange snowflakes.

**Tavern rumor:** The foundry master has been buying every scrap of timber that
comes in. Some say he's building something big in the deep sheds.

**Political stance:** Ironhaven IS the Pact. The Iron Guild's word is law.

**Local rivalry:** Iron Point (West Africa). Both export iron. Ironhaven's is
refined; Iron Point's is raw and cheaper. The foundry masters resent the undercutting.

**Port grudge:** A shipment of Ironhaven weapons was found at Corsair's Rest.
The merchant who sold them was never seen again.

**Trade embargo:** Won't sell weapons to captains who trade with Shadow Ports.

**Exports:** Iron (best source), weapons
**Wants:** Grain, cotton

### 6. Stormwall (North Atlantic)

**Trade bloc:** Iron Pact (enforcer)
**Cultural group:** The Northern Garrison
**Features:** None (military port)

**Identity:** Fortress port guarding the northern straits. Military outpost with
strict inspections. The garrison answers to the Pact, not the crown. Highest
port fees in the game (10 silver) — you're paying for the privilege of being watched.

**Landmark:** The Fortress — grey stone walls rising from the sea, older than anyone remembers.

**Local custom:** All cargo is inspected. Complaining about it marks you as a southerner.

**Dock scene:** Navy vessels line the inner harbor. Soldiers in grey coats patrol
the docks, checking manifests against sealed orders. Everything is orderly.

**Tavern rumor:** The garrison commander has doubled the night watch. Nobody
says why, but the soldiers look north when they think no one's watching.

**Political stance:** Stormwall enforces the Pact's will.

**Port grudge:** The garrison commander's daughter married a trader from
Corsair's Rest. He hasn't spoken to her since.

**Exports:** Medicines
**Wants:** Rum (soldiers love it), tobacco

### 7. Thornport (North Atlantic)

**Trade bloc:** Free Ports
**Cultural group:** The Whaler-Merchants
**Features:** None

**Identity:** Whaling town turned trading post. Tea and tobacco are the local
currency. Too practical for politics — they sell to anyone with silver. The
Whale Arch (a bleached jawbone) spans the harbor entrance.

**Landmark:** The Whale Arch — a jawbone arch spanning the harbor entrance.

**Local custom:** Tea is offered before any negotiation. Tobacco is shared after a deal closes.

**Dock scene:** Whaling boats line the pier. Tea merchants squat beside wooden
crates, brewing samples for prospective buyers.

**Tavern rumor:** The whales are swimming further north each year. The old
captains say the sea is warming.

**Political stance:** Too practical for politics. Tea and tobacco for anyone with silver.

**Port grudge:** Stormwall once tried to tax Thornport's tea trade. Thornport
stopped selling tea to soldiers. The tax lasted two weeks.

**Exports:** Tea, tobacco, timber
**Wants:** Medicines (badly), silk, spice

### 8. Sun Harbor (West Africa)

**Trade bloc:** Gold Coast Compact (voice)
**Cultural group:** The Weighers
**Features:** None

**Identity:** Golden coast port where cotton bales stack higher than the
warehouses. The Weighers — women who count and weigh every bale — speak for
all four Compact ports at trade councils. Their word on quality is final.

**Landmark:** The Cotton Steps — wide stone stairs where cotton bales are displayed and auctioned.

**Local custom:** The first trade of the day is blessed by an elder. Smart captains arrive early.

**Dock scene:** Cotton bales stack three stories high. Women in indigo cloth
oversee the weighing, their voices carrying the count in song.

**Tavern rumor:** A ship arrived last month carrying nothing but empty crates —
paid in full. The harbor master looked the other way.

**Political stance:** Sun Harbor is the Compact's voice.

**Port grudge:** An Iron Pact mining consortium tried to buy Iron Point. The
Compact blocked it. Relations have been cool since.

**Exports:** Cotton (primary), iron, dyes
**Wants:** Silk, spice

### 9. Palm Cove (West Africa)

**Trade bloc:** Gold Coast Compact
**Cultural group:** The Seven Houses
**Features:** None

**Identity:** A sheltered cove where rum barrels outnumber the inhabitants.
Cheapest provisions on the coast. The Seven Houses — seven distillery families
who have been rivals for a century and allies for longer — are the social
fabric of the port.

**Landmark:** Distillery Row — seven rum houses, each with its own secret recipe.

**Local custom:** A cup of rum for every stranger. Refusing is an insult that
lasts generations.

**Dock scene:** Small boats bob in the sheltered cove. Rum barrels roll down
wooden ramps. Children dive for coins tossed by arriving crews.

**Tavern rumor:** Old Cassius at the third distillery claims his rum can cure
fever, heal wounds, and make you invisible to customs inspectors. Two of those
are probably true.

**Political stance:** Palm Cove doesn't do politics. They make rum and share it.

**Port grudge:** None. Palm Cove is incapable of holding a grudge. Cultural impossibility.

**Exports:** Rum (primary), tobacco
**Wants:** Timber

### 10. Iron Point (West Africa)

**Trade bloc:** Gold Coast Compact
**Cultural group:** The Red Hand
**Features:** None

**Identity:** Mining settlement at the river mouth. Iron flows out, everything
else flows in at a premium. The Red Hand — miners whose hands are permanently
stained with iron oxide — are a badge-of-honor collective. They dream of
selling direct to the East, cutting out Ironhaven's markup.

**Landmark:** The River Mouth Mines — tunnels carved into red cliffs.

**Local custom:** Miners spit on their hands before a deal. It means the ore is honest.

**Dock scene:** Ore carts rattle down tracks. The river runs red with mine runoff.
Everything — buildings, clothes, skin — is stained rust.

**Tavern rumor:** The miners hit something in the deep shaft last week. Not iron —
something older. The foreman sealed the tunnel and doubled the guard.

**Political stance:** Iron Point votes with the Compact but dreams of selling direct to the East.

**Local rivalry:** Ironhaven. Same ore, different prices. Iron Point sells raw;
Ironhaven sells refined at triple the markup.

**Port grudge:** Ironhaven called Iron Point ore "unrefined swamp metal" at a
trade fair. The Red Hand has a long memory.

**Exports:** Iron (primary)
**Wants:** Grain, porcelain, weapons (mining tools)

---

### 11. Pearl Shallows (West Africa)

**Trade bloc:** Gold Coast Compact
**Cultural group:** The Breath-Holders
**Features:** None

**Identity:** Divers bring up pearls from the warm shallows. A quiet port where
fortunes are made by the patient. The Breath-Holders — pearl divers who can
hold their breath for impossible minutes — consider their craft sacred.

**Landmark:** The Blessing Pool — a tidal pool where divers pray before descending.

**Local custom:** Pearls are never haggled over. The diver names the price. You pay or you leave.

**Dock scene:** Canoes glide in from the reef. Divers emerge glistening, pouches
of pearls tied to their wrists. An elder examines each pearl by sunlight.

**Tavern rumor:** The oldest diver says there's a pearl the size of a fist
somewhere in the deep reef. Three divers have gone looking. Two came back.

**Local rivalry:** Typhoon Anchorage. Both export pearls. The Breath-Holders
consider Typhoon Anchorage divers reckless. The feeling is mutual.

**Port grudge:** Typhoon Anchorage divers poached Pearl Shallows' reef three
seasons ago. The Breath-Holders haven't spoken to the Storm Riders since.

**Exports:** Pearls (rare), dyes, cotton
**Wants:** Medicines

---

### 12. Jade Port (East Indies)

**Trade bloc:** Silk Circle (elder)
**Cultural group:** The Kiln Masters
**Features:** None

**Identity:** Porcelain workshops line the waterfront. The Kiln Masters have
shaped Eastern trade policy for a thousand years. Their guild marks are more
valued than the clay itself. Iron and grain are worth their weight in gold here.

**Landmark:** The Porcelain Quarter — workshops where master potters fire kilns day and night.

**Local custom:** Porcelain is presented on silk, never bare-handed. Fingerprints void a sale.

**Dock scene:** Bamboo cranes lift crates padded with straw. Each piece of
porcelain is inspected by guild markers before it leaves the dock.

**Tavern rumor:** A master potter smashed his entire year's work because he
found a single flaw. The guild praised him.

**Local rivalry:** Silk Haven. Silk vs. porcelain — which is the supreme art?
The weavers and potters have argued for centuries.

**Port grudge:** A Western merchant once dropped a porcelain bowl. The Kiln
Masters banned his entire family from trade for three generations.

**Exports:** Porcelain (primary), silk, tea
**Wants:** Grain, iron

---

### 13. Monsoon Reach (East Indies)

**Trade bloc:** Free Ports
**Cultural group:** The Monsoon Brotherhood (monks)
**Features:** Shipyard

**Identity:** Seasonal winds funnel the spice trade through this crossroads.
The Wind Temple monks track the monsoon with uncanny accuracy. Their forecasts
guide every departure. Monsoon Reach trades with everyone and judges no one.

**Landmark:** The Wind Temple — a pagoda on the headland where monks track the monsoon.

**Local custom:** Captains consult the monks before sailing. Their forecasts are uncannily accurate.

**Dock scene:** Harbor curves around a headland crowned by the Wind Temple.
Ships here have deeper keels and stronger masts — the monsoon demands it.

**Tavern rumor:** The monks say this monsoon season will be the worst in a
generation. The shipwrights are quietly raising their prices.

**Political stance:** Trades with everyone and judges no one.

**Port grudge:** The monks predicted a typhoon that saved a Silk Circle fleet.
The Circle offered alliance. The monks declined. "We read the wind, not politics."

**Exports:** Spice, silk
**Wants:** Cotton, timber

### 14. Silk Haven (East Indies)

**Trade bloc:** Silk Circle
**Cultural group:** The Silk Weavers' Guild
**Features:** None

**Identity:** Premier silk market of the eastern waters. The Silk Weavers' Guild
guards family patterns passed down for centuries. Rum and iron are scarce
luxuries here. Most expensive crew in the game (8 silver).

**Landmark:** The Loom Quarter — silk woven in patterns a thousand years old.

**Local custom:** Silk is presented folded, never rolled. Rolling insults the weaver's art.

**Dock scene:** Sampans glide between junks with crimson sails. Merchants in silk
robes examine bolts with magnifying glasses, judging thread count by touch.

**Tavern rumor:** The eldest weaver has created a pattern that changes color in
different light. Three merchants have bid their entire fortunes for a single bolt.

**Local rivalry:** Jade Port. Silk vs. porcelain — the eternal debate.

**Port grudge:** Jade Port once called silk "decorated thread." The Guild responded
by weaving a tapestry depicting the Kiln Masters' ancestors as monkeys. It hangs
in the Loom Quarter to this day.

**Exports:** Silk (best source), porcelain
**Wants:** Rum (badly), dyes

### 19. Typhoon Anchorage (South Seas)

**Trade bloc:** Coral Crown
**Cultural group:** The Storm Riders

**Identity:** Storm-battered harbor that only the boldest captains visit. The

**Landmark:** The Storm Wall — breakwater built from the hulls of wrecked ships.

**Dock scene:** Harbor carved into the leeward cliff. Ships are chained, not

**Tavern rumor:** A captain carved her name into the Storm Wall seven times —
one for each typhoon survived. They call her the Unkillable. She drinks alone.

**Local rivalry:** Pearl Shallows. Both export pearls. The Storm Riders dive deeper
and in worse conditions. The Breath-Holders say they lack the sacred technique.

**Port grudge:** Pearl Shallows accused Typhoon Anchorage of reef poaching. The
Storm Riders responded by diving the deepest reef in the South Seas and bringing
up a pearl the size of a fist. Case closed.

**Exports:** Pearls (best source), timber (tropical hardwood)
**Wants:** Silk, porcelain
