# Portlight: Print-and-Play Merchant Adventure Game

A competitive route-and-trade game for 2-4 players. Read markets, race contracts, manage reputation and customs heat, and decide when to play honest versus dangerous.

**Players:** 2-4 | **Duration:** ~90 minutes | **Ages:** 14+

Solo and cooperative variants may follow if the print-and-play gains traction.

---

## Components

- 1 game board (port map with routes)
- 3 captain cards (player boards with tracks)
- 5 ship cards
- 20 port reference cards
- ~50 goods cards (17 types x 3 copies)
- ~24 contract cards
- ~40 event cards
- 4 season cards
- 1 player aid card
- Score track sheets (silver, reputation, heat, trust)
- Tokens/markers (use coins, cubes, or whatever you have)

**You will need:** 2 six-sided dice, pencils, and something to use as player tokens.

---

## Setup

1. Place the game board in the center of the table.
2. Each player chooses a captain card. Take the matching starting silver, provisions, and ship (Sloop).
3. Place your token at your captain's home port.
4. Set your tracks: Silver (per captain card), Reputation 0, Heat (per captain card), Trust at Unproven.
5. Shuffle the goods deck, contract deck, and event deck separately.
6. Deal 3-4 goods cards face-up at each port marked on the board (this is the market display).
7. Deal 4 contracts face-up to the contract board area.
8. Place the Spring season card face-up. Seasons change every 3 rounds.

---

## Captain Archetypes

### Merchant (starts at Porto Novo)
- **Starting silver:** 25 | **Provisions:** 6
- **Ability:** +1 silver on all sell transactions. Contracts cost 1 fewer trust tier to accept.
- **Weakness:** Heat penalties are doubled.

### Navigator (starts at Monsoon Reach)
- **Starting silver:** 20 | **Provisions:** 5
- **Ability:** +1 movement point. May peek at the top event card before choosing a route.
- **Weakness:** Sees 1 fewer contract on the board (only 3 visible to you).

### Smuggler (starts at Corsair's Rest)
- **Starting silver:** 15 | **Provisions:** 4
- **Ability:** May buy and sell contraband goods. On inspections, roll 2 dice and take the better result.
- **Weakness:** Starts with Heat 3. Cannot accept Lawful contracts until trust reaches Credible.

## Round Structure

Each round has 4 phases, played in order by all players simultaneously (or in turn order for contested actions).

### Phase 1: Port

If you are at a port, you may do any combination of the following:

- **Buy goods** from the port's market display. Pay the price printed on the card, modified by regional demand (shown on the port reference card). You may hold goods cards up to your ship's cargo capacity.
- **Sell goods** to the port. Receive the sell price shown. If you sell 3+ of the same good in one round, reduce the price by 2 silver per extra unit (flood penalty).
- **Take a contract** from the contract board. You must meet the trust requirement. You may hold up to 3 active contracts.
- **Buy a ship** if the port has a Shipyard (marked on the board). Sell your current ship for half its cost, then pay full price for the new one.
- **Buy infrastructure** (Warehouse, Broker Office, or License) at this port.
- **Repair your ship** — pay 1 silver per hull point restored.
- **Provision** — pay 1 silver per 2 provisions gained.

### Phase 2: Sail

Choose a destination connected to your current port by a route on the board.

- Each route has a **movement cost** (1-4, printed on the board).
- Your ship provides **movement points** per round: Sloop 4, Cutter 5, Brigantine 3, Galleon 2, Man-of-War 1.
- If the route cost exceeds your remaining movement, place your ship token on the route. You are "at sea" and will continue next round. While at sea, skip the Port phase.
- **Provisions:** Spend 1 provision per round of travel. If you run out, lose 1 silver per round (desperate foraging).
- **Season modifier:** Check the current season card. Some regions have +1 route cost during specific seasons.

### Phase 3: Encounter

Every player who sailed this round (including those still at sea) draws 1 event card and resolves it immediately.

**Event types:**
- **Weather:** May grant bonus movement, damage your hull, or block a route this round.
- **Pirates:** Choose to Fight, Pay Tribute, or Flee (see Combat below).
- **Inspections:** Roll for customs check (see Inspections below).
- **Trade:** Market bonuses, rumors, or discount opportunities.
- **Culture:** Regional events with one-time bonuses or penalties.

At **Heat 5+**, draw an additional Inspection event (on top of your normal event card).

### Phase 4: Resolution

1. **Complete contracts:** If you are at the destination port of an active contract and have the required goods in your cargo, deliver them. Gain the reward silver and +1 Trust.
2. **Tick contracts:** All active contracts lose 1 from their deadline counter. If any reach 0, the contract fails: lose 1 Trust and pay the penalty.
3. **Heat decay:** If you gained no heat this round, reduce Heat by 1 (minimum 0).
4. **Infrastructure upkeep:** Pay 1 silver per infrastructure piece you own.
5. **Crew upkeep:** Pay your ship's crew cost in silver.
6. **Refill markets:** Draw goods cards to refill each port's market display to 3-4 cards.
7. **Refill contracts:** Draw contracts to refill the contract board to 4 cards.
8. **Advance season:** Every 3 rounds, flip to the next season card (Spring > Summer > Autumn > Winter > Spring).

## Ships

| Ship | Cargo | Speed | Hull | Crew Cost | Price |
|------|-------|-------|------|-----------|-------|
| Sloop | 5 cards | 4 | 6 | 0/round | Free (start) |
| Cutter | 7 cards | 5 | 7 | 1/round | 20 silver |
| Brigantine | 10 cards | 3 | 10 | 2/round | 40 silver |
| Galleon | 15 cards | 2 | 16 | 3/round | 80 silver |
| Man-of-War | 20 cards | 1 | 22 | 4/round | 150 silver |

**Cargo** = maximum goods cards you can hold.
**Speed** = movement points per round.
**Hull** = damage threshold. At 0, your ship is wrecked (lose all cargo, return to nearest port, start repairs).
**Crew Cost** = silver paid each round during Resolution.

Ships can only be purchased at ports with a **Shipyard** (marked [S] on the board). Sell your old ship for half price when upgrading.

## Goods

Goods are cards you buy at ports and sell at others for profit. Each card shows:
- **Name and type** (Commodity, Luxury, Provision, Military, Contraband)
- **Base price** in silver
- **Weight** (most goods = 1 cargo slot; some contraband = 0.5)

**Regional demand:** Port reference cards show which goods each port produces (buy cheap) and consumes (sell high). The difference is your profit margin.

**Contraband** (Opium, Black Powder, Stolen Cargo): Only available at Black Market ports (marked [B]). High margins but increase Heat when carried. Only the Smuggler captain can trade these.

---

## Contracts

Contract cards show:
- **Goods required** and **quantity**
- **Destination port**
- **Deadline** (rounds remaining)
- **Reward** (silver)
- **Trust requirement** (Unproven / New / Credible / Reliable / Trusted)

Take a contract during the Port phase if you meet the trust requirement. Deliver the goods to the destination before the deadline to earn the reward. Failed contracts reduce Trust.

**Trust ladder:** Unproven (start) > New (1 contract) > Credible (3 contracts) > Reliable (6 contracts) > Trusted (10 contracts). Higher tiers unlock premium contracts with bigger rewards.

## Reputation and Heat

Two tracks on your player board, each 0-20.

### Reputation
- **+1** per contract completed
- **+1** per first lawful trade at a new port (once per port)
- Required for some infrastructure and victory paths

### Heat (Customs Attention)
- **+1** per contraband good in your cargo (checked each round)
- **+1** per luxury good sold above 10 silver profit margin
- **+2** per failed inspection
- **-1** per round if you gained no heat (natural decay)
- **-2** per round spent at a Safe Harbor port doing nothing

**Heat consequences:**
- **5+:** Draw an extra Inspection event each Encounter phase
- **10+:** When arriving at a port, roll 2d6. On 6 or less, denied entry (skip Port phase, must sail elsewhere)
- **15+:** All sell prices reduced by 3 silver

## Combat and Inspections

### Pirate Encounter
When you draw a Pirate event, choose one:
- **Fight:** Roll 2d6 + your ship's cannons (Sloop 0, Cutter 1, Brigantine 3, Galleon 6, Man-of-War 10). Beat the pirate's strength (printed on the card) to win. Victory: take a reward. Failure: lose 1-3 cargo cards and 2 hull.
- **Pay Tribute:** Lose silver equal to the pirate's demand (printed on the card).
- **Flee:** Roll 2d6. Beat (12 - your ship's speed) to escape. Success: no penalty. Failure: lose 1 cargo card and 1 hull.

### Inspection
Roll 2d6 minus your Heat (to a minimum of 2).
- **7+:** Passed. No consequences.
- **Below 7:** Seized. Lose all contraband goods in your cargo. Heat +2.
- **Smuggler advantage:** Roll 2d6, take the better die, then subtract Heat.

## Infrastructure

Buy these at any port during the Port phase:

| Type | Cost | Upkeep | Effect |
|------|------|--------|--------|
| Warehouse | 15 silver | 1/round | Store up to 5 goods cards at this port between visits |
| Broker Office | 25 silver | 1/round | +1 silver on all trades in this port's region |
| License | 10 silver | 1/round | Access restricted contracts and goods in this region |

Limited to 1 of each per port. Infrastructure persists until you stop paying upkeep (then it's abandoned).

---

## Seasons

Every 3 rounds, the season advances:

| Season | Rounds | Effect |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Spring | 1-3 | Mediterranean routes -1 cost (min 1). Grain demand +2. |
| Summer | 4-6 | East Indies routes +1 cost (monsoon). Spice demand +2. |
| Autumn | 7-9 | West Africa routes stable. Cotton and Dyes demand +2. |
| Winter | 10-12 | North Atlantic routes +1 cost (storms). Medicines demand +2. |

After round 12, seasons cycle back to Spring if the game continues.

## Victory

**The game ends immediately when any player completes a victory path.** That player wins.

If no one wins by the end of round 15, the player with the highest score wins:
**Score = Silver + (Reputation x 2) - Heat**

### Victory Paths

**Lawful Trade House**
- Trust at Trusted (10+ contracts completed)
- Reputation 12+
- Heat 3 or less
- 3+ contracts completed this game
- 50+ silver

**Shadow Network**
- 2+ contraband deliveries completed
- Heat sustained at 8+ for 3 consecutive rounds (track this)
- 40+ silver
- Survived a seizure (had goods seized and kept playing)

**Oceanic Reach**
- Visited ports in 4+ different regions
- Own a Brigantine or better
- Completed a contract with destination in East Indies
- 40+ silver

**Commercial Empire**
- Infrastructure in 3+ different regions
- Trust at Reliable (6+ contracts)
- 3+ contracts completed this game
- 60+ silver

## Quick Reference

**Turn order:** Port > Sail > Encounter > Resolution

**Buy goods:** Pay base price (modified by port demand)
**Sell goods:** Receive sell price. Flood penalty if 3+ same good.
**Take contract:** Must meet trust tier. Max 3 active.
**Repair:** 1 silver per hull point.
**Provision:** 1 silver per 2 provisions.

**Movement:** Ship speed = movement points. Route cost = points spent.
**At sea:** Skip Port phase, still draw Encounter cards.

**Heat 5+ =** extra inspection. **Heat 10+ =** port denial risk. **Heat 15+ =** sell penalty.

**Contract fail:** Deadline reaches 0. Lose 1 Trust, pay penalty.
**Ship wreck:** Hull at 0. Lose all cargo, return to nearest port.
